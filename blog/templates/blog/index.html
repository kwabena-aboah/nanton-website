  • filter_dramaCentral Admin
    Central Administration is headed by the District Coordinating Director (DCD) and is responsible for ensuring the implementation of governmental policies, projects and programmes at the MMDAs level.
    The department guides policy formulation, planning and decision-making at the District Assembly.
    As part of it's responsibilities, the Central Administration acts as a liaison between Heads of other governmental and non-governmental agencies and the District Chief Executive (DCE). It manages financial and other resources of the Assembly in consultation with the DCE.
  • filter_dramaFinance Department
    The Finance Department leads in the management and use of financial resources to achieve value for money. This department advices management on the Financial Administration Act, Internal Audit Agency Act, Procurement Act and any other financial regulations approved by Government and by doing so ensures the maintenance of proper accounts.
    It directs and controls financial management in line with public sector accounting principles thereby safeguarding of the District Assembly's assets.
  • filter_dramaEducation
    The Education, Youth and Sports Unit primarily assists in the formulation and implementation of policies on education in the Assembly within the framework of national policies and guidelines.
    The department as an advisory role in the District on matters relating to pre-school, primary, junior high schools in the Assembly and other matters that may be referred to it by the Assembly.
    The Education department also encourages and reports on implementation of policies and matters relating to basic education in accordance with repor format provided by the minister.
